# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in this project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

**Positive behavior includes:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members

**Unacceptable behavior includes:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and may take corrective action — including removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, issues, or other contributions — in response to behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions) and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a private GitHub Discussion or contacting the maintainer directly. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated and will result in a response deemed necessary and appropriate. Maintainers are obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter.
